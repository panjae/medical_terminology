V. TYPES OF SMOOTH MUSCLE

A. PHASIC SMOOTH MUSCLE
Single Unit
ICC Pacemaker (3-20 cycles/min)
Connection: Gap junctions (electrical coupling)
Function: Single unit, coordinated
Contraction: Transient, rhythmic
Speed: Fast (10-20x slower than skeletal)
AP: L-type Ca²⁺ mediated, BK modulated
GI tract: walls
Urogenital: ureters, bladder
Reproductive: uterus, vas deferens
Vessels: small arteries

Multi-Unit

Connection: Isolated cells, no gap junctions
Function: Multi-unit, independent control
Contraction: Sustained, graded
Speed: Slow (optimized for sustained force)
AP: Usually no AP, graded response
Eye: iris, ciliary muscle
Vessels: large arteries, veins
Sphincters: LES, pyloric
Airways: bronchioles
Electrical Properties
Phasic: Vm oscillations, slow waves from ICC pacemakers, propagated APs
Tonic: Stable Vm, graded depolarization, receptor-operated responses
Gene Expression
Phasic: Fast MLC/MHC isoforms, ↑MLCK, ↑MYPT1
Tonic: Slow MLC/MHC isoforms, optimized for efficiency
Functional Roles
Phasic: Peristalsis, mixing, propulsion, rhythmic contractions
Tonic: Maintain tone, fine control, prolonged contractions
