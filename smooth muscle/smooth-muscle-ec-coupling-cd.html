Chapter 13. III. EXCITATION-CONTRACTION COUPLING

C. Relaxation & D. Latchbridge Formation

Muscle relaxation occurs when sarcoplasmic Ca²⁺ concentrations renormalize. Because smooth muscle contraction involves a kinase and phosphorylation step, relaxation requires a phosphatase. Unique to smooth muscle is the latchbridge state, allowing sustained contraction with minimal ATP use (~1% of skeletal muscle requirement).

C. Relaxation

1. Ca²⁺ Renormalization

  • Ca²⁺ extrusion:
    • Ca²⁺-ATPases (PMCA)
    • Na⁺-Ca²⁺ exchangers (NCX)
    • Remove Ca²⁺ from cell
  • SR reuptake:
    • SERCA pumps
    • ATP-dependent
    • Restores SR stores
  • MLCK deactivates when [Ca²⁺] falls

2. Store Refill

  • SOC mechanism:
    • Store-operated Ca²⁺ channel
    • Stim1 senses depletion
    • Signals to Orai (SOC subunit)
    • Sustained Ca²⁺ influx for refill
  • Occurs at SR-sarcolemma junctions
  • Tops off depleted stores

3. Dephosphorylation

  • MLCP components:
    • PP1c (37-kDa catalytic)
    • MYPT1 (110-130 kDa targeting)
    • 20-kDa subunit
    • Constitutively active
  • Always opposing MLCK
  • Strips phosphate from MLC20
  • Essential for relaxation

D. Latchbridge Formation

Unique to smooth muscle: sustained force with minimal ATP use

Mechanism

  • Myosin dephosphorylated while attached to actin
  • Locks or "latches" in place
  • Intrinsically slow cycling rate
  • Maintains tension with minimal energy

Characteristics

  • Energy efficiency:
    • ~1% of skeletal muscle ATP
    • Same tension maintained
    • Hours of sustained contraction
  • Physiologic importance:
    • Blood vessel tone
    • Sphincter function
    • Hollow organ maintenance
  • Requires minimal Ca²⁺ elevation
  • Can only occur during contraction

Latchbridge Cycle

Phosphorylated myosin binds actin
→
MLCP dephosphorylates attached myosin
→
"Latched" state (slow cycling)
→
Sustained force

💪 Skeletal Muscle

ATP consumption for sustained contraction:

100% ATP

• Rapid crossbridge cycling
• High energy demand
• Cannot sustain for hours

🌊 Smooth Muscle (Latchbridge)

ATP consumption for same tension:

~1%

• Slow/locked crossbridges
• Minimal energy use
• Hours of sustained tone

💡 Fundamental Property: Latchbridge State

The latchbridge mechanism is a unique and fundamental property of smooth muscle that enables prolonged contractions essential for physiologic functions. When myosin is dephosphorylated while still attached to actin, it becomes "locked" in place with an intrinsically slow cycling rate. This allows blood vessels to maintain tone, sphincters to stay closed, and hollow organs to sustain pressure for hours with only ~1% of the ATP that skeletal muscle would require. This remarkable energy efficiency is critical for organs that must maintain continuous tension without fatigue.
