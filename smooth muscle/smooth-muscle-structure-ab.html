Chapter 13. II. STRUCTURE

A. Contractile Units & B. Assembly

Smooth muscle develops force through actin-myosin interactions. Myosin concentrations are much lower than in skeletal muscle, but actin levels are similar. Individual cells contain minisarcomeres - small contractile units that lack regular alignment, resulting in no visible striations ("smooth" muscle).

üí° Key Structure-Function Relationship

The unique arrangement of minisarcomeres anchored by dense bodies and connected via intermediate filaments allows smooth muscle to contract in multiple directions and maintain tension over wide length ranges (50-400% of resting length). This makes it ideal for hollow organs that undergo significant volume changes.
